# GTD Effectiveness (early impressions)

I am roughly a month into running a real GTD practice — capture, weekly review, the whole flow — and I want to write down some impressions while they are still fresh. The honest disclaimer up front: it is too early to tell. A month of data is barely a data point, and I have been keeping the practice through a relatively calm patch of work, so I have not stress-tested it against a genuinely chaotic week. Take everything below as a tentative read.

## What seems to be working

Capture has felt like the most immediately rewarding piece. I am not sure yet whether that is because capture itself reduces cognitive load, or whether I was previously underestimating how much background noise was generated by trying to remember things. Either way, the inbox-as-pressure-release-valve effect appears real for me, with the caveat that I have been a touch lax about clarifying captured items within a day, so the inbox tends to bloat by Thursday.

Early trials suggest the weekly review might be the keystone habit, but I want to be careful about that conclusion. It could just be that the act of sitting down for an hour on a Sunday with my notes would be useful with or without the GTD scaffolding around it. See [[weekly-review-protocol]] for the version I have been running.

## What I am unsure about

I do not yet have a feel for whether the project list works for the kind of work I do. Some of my projects are very short — two or three actions — and others stretch over months, and the same list format may not serve both. I might split them, but I want to give it another month before I tweak.

This could be useful in certain contexts and less so in others; I think I will know better by summer.
