# Knowledge Tools

This is the entry point for everything I have written about how I capture, store, and retrieve knowledge over time. The questions that drive these notes: which tools survive a year of daily use, and which of the popular practices are signal versus aesthetic?

## What lives here

The capture-and-retrieve philosophy lives in [[atomic-notes-principle]] and [[zettelkasten-vs-folders]]. The first is the unit of capture I have converged on; the second is the organisational stance I have settled into after several years of trying both extremes.

The tool selection lives in [[why-obsidian]] and [[markdown-portability]]. These two are linked: I chose Obsidian largely because it gets out of the way of plain-text portability, and the portability is what gives me confidence to invest long-term in the format.

The technique notes are [[wikilinks-vs-tags]], [[spaced-repetition-utility]], [[note-density-tradeoff]], and [[search-vs-browse]]. Each documents a question I had to resolve experimentally, with the answers being more nuanced than the loud opinions on the internet suggested.

The lab notebook for what I am building right now is [[this-vaultnexus-experiment]] — a note about the very tool that indexes this vault. Slightly recursive, but knowledge tools that cannot examine themselves are knowledge tools I do not fully trust.

## What does not live here

Habits that operate on the notes — the weekly review, capture rituals — live in the productivity cluster. The notes-as-decision-substrate question lives in the decisions cluster.

## Open threads

A long-running question I do not have a satisfying answer to: at what vault size does manual organisation stop being worth it, and the tooling has to take over? My current vault is around the size where it matters, and the tooling I am building lives in that question.
