# Atomic Notes Principle

The atomic-notes idea, from the Zettelkasten tradition and refined by Sönke Ahrens and others, is that each note should capture a single idea well enough that it can be linked to and reasoned about independently. The principle sounds obvious; the practice is harder than it sounds, and getting it right has changed how my whole knowledge system functions.

## What "one idea" actually means

The first time I read about atomic notes I assumed it meant short notes. That is wrong. An atomic note can be five hundred words; what makes it atomic is that you cannot meaningfully decompose it without losing the idea. A note titled "GTD" is not atomic — it is a topic, and the topic contains many ideas. A note titled "Why the weekly review is the load-bearing ritual in GTD" is atomic, because its central claim cannot be split further without breaking it.

The test I use: can I write a one-sentence summary of the note that captures what it is *for*? If yes, atomic. If the one-sentence summary is just "stuff about X," the note is a folder masquerading as a thinking unit.

## Why it matters for linking

Linking only works if the link targets are precise. Linking to "GTD" tells me nothing about why the linker pointed there. Linking to "Why the weekly review is the load-bearing ritual" tells me the claim being referenced, and I can disagree with the link or agree with it on substance. The atomic structure is what makes the link load-bearing rather than decorative.

## Why it changes the writing process

Atomic notes change writing because they shift the unit of finished work. A traditional essay is finished when the essay is finished. A vault of atomic notes is never finished; instead, individual claims accumulate and link, and the essay falls out of selecting and ordering the relevant atoms. I get more useful work out of two atomic notes than out of one half-finished essay.

## The trap

The trap is over-atomising. If every sentence becomes a note, the graph fragments and the links lose meaning because there is no resistance to traversal. The discipline is to keep notes atomic at the level of *claim*, not at the level of *fact*.
