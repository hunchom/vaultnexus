# GTD Overview

Getting Things Done is, at its core, a five-step loop: capture, clarify, organise, reflect, engage. Most of the trouble people have with it is not philosophical disagreement — it is that they only practise two or three of the five steps and then conclude the system is broken when the omitted steps would have caught the breakage.

## The five steps as I run them

Capture is everything. Anything that pulls on my attention — a half-formed worry, an email I cannot answer right now, a person I owe a reply to — goes into a single inbox. I do not try to triage at capture time; that is the next step's job. The inbox can be a paper notebook, a notes app, a voice memo, an email folder. What matters is that capture is frictionless. See [[inbox-zero-strategy]] for the discharge ritual.

Clarify happens at most twice a day. For each captured item I ask: is this actionable? If yes, what is the very next physical action? If no, do I trash it, file it as reference, or hold it on a someday/maybe list? This is the step most people skip and then wonder why their inbox feels heavy.

Organise places clarified items into the right list — next-actions by context, projects (anything requiring more than one action), waiting-for (things blocked on others), and the calendar (anything date-locked).

Reflect is the weekly review. See [[weekly-review-protocol]] for the protocol I actually run.

Engage is the part where I do the work, ideally inside a [[deep-work-blocks]] container.

## The version I run

I keep the spirit and ditch some of the ceremony. I do not maintain separate context lists (`@phone`, `@errands`) because my work no longer fragments along physical-context lines. The next-action list is one list, sorted by project. The weekly review is non-negotiable; everything else flexes.
