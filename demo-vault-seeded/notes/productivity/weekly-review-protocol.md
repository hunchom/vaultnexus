# Weekly Review Protocol

The weekly review is the load-bearing ritual of my whole working life. I run it every Sunday afternoon, roughly forty-five minutes, with a coffee and no notifications. The version below is the one I have converged on after a couple of years of experimentation.

## The fixed agenda

I follow the same agenda every week, in the same order. The order matters less than the consistency; what I am buying with consistency is the ability to do the review on autopilot when I am tired or unmotivated.

First, inbox processing. Every captured item from the past week gets clarified — actionable or not, what is the very next physical action, which project does it belong to. Anything still pending in the email inbox follows the same treatment.

Second, project review. I walk down the project list. For each active project I ask: is there a next action defined, is anything blocked, and is the project still worth being active. Roughly one in four reviews kills a project I had been keeping alive out of inertia.

Third, calendar look-back and look-forward. I scan the past week's calendar — did I do what I said I would, anything I want to remember — and the coming week, scanning for collisions with the maker-day rules in [[maker-vs-manager]].

Fourth, someday/maybe and reference. A quick pass, mostly to remind myself what is there.

## Why I no longer skip it

I skipped it twice in the early months. Both following weeks were measurably worse. I caught fewer dropped balls, I felt more reactive, and by Wednesday I was running on a stale picture of my own commitments. After the second skip I stopped treating the review as optional. It is the appointment I keep with myself.

## What I do when I cannot do it on Sunday

Travel weeks happen. The fallback is a thirty-minute version on Monday morning, before email. Skipping entirely is the failure mode; a compressed version is fine.
