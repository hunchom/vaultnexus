# Personal Blog Growth

The bet I am marking: if I commit to publishing 30 essays over the next 24 months — averaging slightly more than one per month — my personal blog will reach 5,000 monthly active readers by November 2026.

## The reasoning behind the number

5,000 MAU is a meaningful threshold for me personally: it is the point where the audience is large enough to produce regular meaningful reader feedback and small enough to feel like a real community rather than a broadcast. It is also, based on what I have seen from other writers in adjacent niches, the threshold that the consistency-of-30-essays input tends to produce. Not always — there is heavy variance — but often enough that it is a reasonable median expectation.

The 30-essay input is the actually-falsifiable part. If I publish 12 essays in 24 months and miss the audience target, the forecast is partially uninformative because the input was not met. If I publish 30+ and miss, the forecast was wrong on its merits.

## The mechanism I am betting on

I am betting that consistent output beats clever distribution at this scale, partly because I am bad at distribution and partly because I have seen the consistent-output pattern work for writers I respect. The compounding here is slow but real: each essay seeds discovery of the previous essays, and the back-catalogue does more reader-acquisition work than the new release in the long run.

## How I could be wrong

I might be wrong because the open web is a worse audience-acquisition channel in 2026 than it was in 2020, and the 5,000 MAU number was calibrated against the older channel. I might be wrong because my topic mix does not have an audience of 5,000 at this depth. Or I might publish 30 essays and discover that they are not, in retrospect, my best work — the consistency input was met but the quality input was below threshold.

## Resolution criteria

End-of-period readout from blog analytics, taken as the trailing-30-day MAU on November 1, 2026.
