# AI Capabilities by End of 2027

Putting this on record so future-me can grade it. The bet I am willing to make today, with the information I have, is that by end of 2027 we will have AI assistants that reliably complete multi-step research tasks at a level comparable to a competent junior analyst.

## What I mean by "reliably"

I mean: given a brief like "investigate the regulatory environment for X in three jurisdictions and produce a five-page memo with citations," the assistant produces, on the first attempt, a memo that a senior reviewer would judge as acceptable junior-analyst work in at least 70% of cases. Not perfect work — junior-analyst work, with the same kinds of errors a junior analyst would make.

This rules out the current state of the art, where the same prompt produces fluent-sounding output that requires substantial verification and re-prompting. The bar is end-to-end usefulness, not impressive demos.

## Why I think it lands

The trajectory from 2022 to 2024 was steeper than I expected. The compounding pattern is: each capability tier unlocks new tooling, the new tooling unlocks new training data, and the new training data unlocks the next capability tier. The current ceiling is dominated by tool use and long-horizon coherence, both of which are areas of intense, well-funded effort.

## Why I might be wrong

The most likely way I am wrong is that the failure modes turn out to be qualitatively different from the ones I am extrapolating from — that long-horizon coherence requires algorithmic innovations that have not yet been invented, not just scale. There is also the boring failure mode where progress continues but commercial deployment lags behind capability by enough that "reliable assistant" never quite arrives in the user experience.

## Resolution criteria

I will judge this at the end of 2027 by trying to use the best publicly available assistant to do exactly the kind of multi-step research task described above, and recording whether the first-shot output is acceptable. The judgement will be qualitative but written-down.
