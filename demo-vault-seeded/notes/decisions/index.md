# Decision Making

This is the entry point for everything I have written about how I make and review decisions. The questions that drive these notes: which of my decisions are dominated by intuition versus analysis, and which kind ought to be dominated by which?

## What lives here

The core notes here are about the meta-question of decision style. [[intuition-vs-analysis]] is the framing I keep returning to. [[ooda-loop-fast]] is the operational loop I use under time pressure. [[premortem-checklist]] is the technique I use when I have the luxury of time, and [[sunk-cost-trap]] documents the most common bias I catch myself committing.

Then there are the live forecasts. I have started marking specific predictions with frontmatter so that future-me can resolve them honestly. The five marked so far are [[ai-capabilities-2027]], [[remote-work-future]], [[personal-blog-growth]], [[skill-acquisition-rate]], and [[regret-minimization-frame]]. Each of those notes carries a single falsifiable claim, a deadline, and the date I marked the prediction. The point is not to be right; the point is to find out, over time, which of my reasoning patterns generates better forecasts than others.

## What does not live here

Operational habits — the things I do every week regardless of whether a decision is on the table — live in the productivity cluster.
