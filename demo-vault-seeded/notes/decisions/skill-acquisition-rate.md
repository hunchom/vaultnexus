# Skill Acquisition Rate

Marking my own learning curve as a forecast so future-me has something to grade. The bet is that 18 months of five-hours-a-week deliberate practice on Rust will get me to working fluency — being able to read and write moderately complex Rust without leaning on documentation for routine syntax.

## The "moderately complex" bar

I mean: I can implement a CLI tool that parses arguments, walks the filesystem, processes data, and writes results, without reaching for the docs every fifteen minutes. I can read a 500-line library and have an accurate model of what it does. I am not betting on being able to implement async runtimes from scratch or design lock-free data structures. That is the next 18 months after this.

## Why I think the timeline lands

The skill-acquisition literature is consistent on a rough order-of-magnitude: ~400 hours of deliberate practice converts a beginner to a competent practitioner in most technical domains, and 5 hours/week × 78 weeks ≈ 390 hours, which is right in the centre of that range. I am also coming in with adjacent expertise — systems programming concepts, type-system intuition from other languages — so the transfer should be positive.

The deliberate-practice phrase is doing work in the prediction. Five hours of grinding through tutorials is not the same as five hours of struggling at the edge of my current ability. The bet is contingent on the practice being deliberate.

## Why I might miss

The most likely failure: practice slips below 5 hours/week. Life is lumpy and a quarter of bad sickness or work crunch could shave the average enough to push the timeline.

The second most likely failure: my "deliberate practice" turns out to be insufficiently deliberate. I have learned languages before by building things at the edge of my comfort, but I have also wasted months on tutorial-treadmill, and the failure mode is hard to detect from inside.

## Resolution

I will judge this in May 2026 by attempting, cold, to read a 500-line Rust project I have not seen before and writing a one-page summary of what it does. If the summary is substantially accurate, the forecast resolves yes.
