# Remote Work Future

Marking this prediction now so I can grade it honestly later. My current read is that the remote-default era of 2021 through 2023 is closing, and by mid-2026 the median knowledge-work role in major tech hubs will look like a three-day-in-office hybrid.

## The signal I am reading

Return-to-office mandates have been accumulating through 2023 and 2024 with a consistent direction even when the specifics vary. The pattern is that the early-pandemic remote experiment is being characterised, in retrospect, as either a productivity win or a productivity loss depending on the role, and the roles where it is being characterised as a loss are exactly the high-leverage roles that the median knowledge worker holds.

I am also tracking a softer signal: the social organisation of careers — mentorship, promotion, network-building — appears to favour in-person work in ways that the pure-output framing misses. Companies that compete for talent will eventually re-encode that advantage into their default policies.

## Why I might be wrong

The most plausible alternative outcome: a bifurcation persists, where high-trust senior workers remain remote-by-default and only the more junior workforce returns to physical presence. The "median worker" framing might wash this out either way depending on how the median lands.

Another way I am wrong: a competitor effect kicks in, where one or two large employers commit to full-remote as a recruiting wedge, and the median shifts back toward remote because top talent flows to those employers.

## Resolution criteria

I will judge this by surveying job postings from the ten largest tech employers in three major hubs as of June 2026, counting the fraction requiring 3+ days in-office, and comparing against the same measurement today. The bar is "median requires 3+ days."
