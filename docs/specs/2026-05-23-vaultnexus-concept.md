# VaultNexus — Design Concept

A local-first knowledge engine for a personal Markdown/Obsidian vault, exposed to Claude Code (and other MCP clients) over the Model Context Protocol. It does best-in-class semantic + graph retrieval over your notes, and reasons over them: it surfaces where distant notes secretly agree, and answers questions by reasoning over the vault with a citation for every step. **That retrieval-and-convergence core is the product** — fast, exact, offset-faithful, local, and what you reach for dozens of times a day. On top of it sits an **epistemic instrument** — measuring how your beliefs drift over time, calibrating your own predictions, and catching genuine self-contradiction — treated as a set of **validated-first experiments**: each ships only if it clears a pre-registered bar, and the product is excellent whether or not any of them do. The honest framing throughout this document: the core earns its keep on day one; the instrument is a bet we validate before we build, not a promise.

**The positioning.** VaultNexus competes on **execution, not category invention**. The daily product — semantic + graph retrieval and cross-community convergence — is commodity *in kind* (`obra/knowledge-graph` ships free convergence + cited reasoning; Smart Connections does neighbor surfacing), so VaultNexus wins it on *engineering*: an int8-SIMD exact engine that is faster, offset-faithful, and never freezes the editor, where the incumbents are slower, approximate, and (Smart Connections) compute in the renderer. That is what earns its keep 50× a day. Durability is a **separate axis** from the daily hook: the one capability no shipping tool offers is a longitudinal *epistemic instrument over your own authored corpus* (drift, calibration, self-contradiction) — but this is an **honest first-mover bet, not a proven moat** (§1 spells out why, including where the earlier "competitors structurally can't" claim was simply wrong). *Not a second brain that talks back; an instrument that measures how you think — on a retrieval core that earns its keep on day one regardless.*

Target scale: a real personal vault — **10⁴–10⁶ chunks** (roughly 1k–200k notes). Single user, single machine, local-first, no Docker.

*Honest effort up front: ~**12–15 person-months** to a validated, polished Tier A (single builder, AI-assisted pace); the critical path is recruiting real third-party vault owners for the validation spikes, not engineering time. The "days" figures in §10 describe only the spikes — never the build.*

**Design stance on risk.** The single load-bearing empirical assumption in this whole concept is that an LLM-judged claim graph over messy personal notes is trustworthy enough that *contradiction detection* clears a hard false-positive bar. That assumption is unproven and can only be settled by running it — not argued from a document. The concept is therefore deliberately **partitioned** into a *spike-independent core* (an MVP of retrieval, convergence, cited reasoning, and belief-drift narration; then a separately-gated Milestone-2 Calibrated-Self instrument — confirmation-drift analytics and the prediction ledger) that is complete and excellent on its own, and a *spike-gated layer* (the Contradiction Sentinel) that is enabled only by a pre-registered empirical result. "Contradiction ships off by default" is treated here as the **expected, planned-for branch**, not a failure. Everything load-bearing for the product's value lives in the core. See §0 (decision tree) and §10 (the spike).

## 0. Risk-bounded structure & decision tree

The product is split into two tiers so that **no spike outcome can sink it**.

**Tier A — the spike-independent core (must be A-grade on its own).** Tier A itself splits along a second seam — the real shippability boundary — into an **MVP** and a separately-sequenced, separately-gated **Milestone-2 instrument**:

- **MVP (ships first, demoable day one):** elite exact retrieval (hierarchical chunk + int8-SIMD exact top-k + BM25 + fusion + the two-lane pipeline), **Convergence/Bridge** detection, **cited reasoning** (`reason_over_vault`), and **belief-drift narration** (`recall_history`). Every item is FP-safe or deterministic and demonstrable on a fresh install against the seeded demo vault (§6).
- **Milestone-2 — the Calibrated-Self instrument (separately sequenced + gated):** confirmation-drift analytics (the durability bet) **plus** the Decision & Prediction Ledger. Their arithmetic is deterministic, but they earn their value *over the user's own corpus across time*, so they are sequenced after the MVP and gated on their own bars (drift on its §10.9 precision + lexicon-stability gate — it is the one not-FP-safe surface; the per-community ledger on a minimum-n + bootstrap-CI — see §4), not on the §10 contradiction spike.

Every Tier-A item is FP-tolerant or self-evidently-correct by construction — with exactly one explicit, separately-gated exception, confirmation-drift (below):
- *Retrieval* is judged by Recall@k / MRR / NDCG against a golden set — a standard, ungameable, well-understood bar. No LLM-judged graph in the path.
- *Convergence* surfaces a *suggested* link; a wrong suggestion costs the user three seconds of reading, never trust. It is FP-safe by design, so it needs no precision kill-criterion.
- *Cited reasoning* attaches a verbatim `path#heading^block` to every hop; the user verifies by clicking. The citation is checkable mechanically (does the quoted span exist at that offset? — a contract test), so the failure mode is "unhelpful," not "confidently wrong and untraceable."
- *Belief-drift narration* is git history + dated frontmatter narrated — deterministic, checkable provenance (every claimed change points at a dated diff the user can open); characterizing *how* a stance shifted is a low-stakes judgment with a small bounded error surface, not a fabrication risk.
- *Confirmation-drift analytics* (Milestone-2, the durability bet) is a deterministic slope — a conviction score from a fixed hedge/assertion lexicon vs. the supporting-claim count, both over git-time on text the user already wrote. The arithmetic is clean, but the lexicon→conviction mapping is an unvalidated model, so this is the **one Tier-A surface that is *not* FP-safe** — the one exception to this list. It is **gated on a pre-registered precision bar (§10.9)** exactly as contradiction is, and ships in Tier A only if it clears that bar (else deferred to a separate milestone or dropped). It also needs enough corpus history to have a slope — the cold-start gap is solved by the seeded demo vault (§6).
- *The Ledger* is date math + a Brier score over the user's own explicitly-marked predictions — arithmetic, not inference, and FP-safe. The **global** Brier ships in Milestone-2; the **per-community** breakdown is gated behind a minimum-n + bootstrap-CI so tiny per-community samples are never quoted as if precise (§4, §10.10).

None of these depends on the contradiction thesis. **Tier A is the product.** It is designed to merit an A-grade RISK on its own, shipped with the contradiction feature absent entirely.

**Tier B — the spike-gated layer (the Contradiction Sentinel).** The only component whose correctness rides on the trustworthiness of an LLM-judged claim graph. It is built **last**, behind a gate, and is the only thing the §10 spike can switch off.

### The flank the contradiction gate does NOT cover

The tree above de-risks the Tier-B *bonus*. But it bounds only one flank. The single keystone assumption holding up this whole concept is not "contradiction is precise" — that is gated. It is **"Tier A is the product, and Tier A is genuinely elite/desirable on its own."** Every defense of Tier A in §0 and §4 is a *correctness/safety* argument — FP-safe-by-construction, mechanically-checkable citations, arithmetic over user data. **Not one of them is a desirability argument.** Correctness is necessary but not sufficient for "A-grade": a tool can be provably FP-safe, deterministically correct, and still be something no one opens twice. The elite-vs-merely-correct outcome lives entirely inside an assumption the document currently never measures. So Tier A gets its own pre-registered gate — a **value spike** (§10.8) run *before* the full build commit — and the two not-FP-safe epistemic judgments inside Tier A each get an explicit precision gate, exactly as contradiction does.

## 1. Problem

Existing tools leave a narrowing gap. Smart Connections computes embeddings inside Obsidian's renderer and freezes the app; it does cosine-neighbor surfacing only. Obsidian MCP servers are thin CRUD-over-REST wrappers. Topic-level gap-finders (e.g. InfraNodus) work at the topic level, not the claim level. And as of March 2026 the gap is partly closed: `obra/knowledge-graph` already does community/bridge convergence and cited multi-hop reasoning over a vault, free. So convergence and cited reasoning are no longer the opportunity — they are commodity we intend to *out-engineer*, not own. The remaining unclaimed ground is **longitudinal, quantitative epistemic measurement of the author against their own corpus over time** — confirmation-drift and per-domain calibration — which no shipping tool does and which (§1) is unoccupied today — an honest first-mover bet, not something structurally hard for competitors to add.

Crucially, the moat does **not** rest on contradiction detection — and just as crucially, it does **not** rest on convergence or cited reasoning either. As of March 2026, `obra/knowledge-graph` (Jesse Vincent) ships free as a Claude Code plugin with `kg_communities` (Louvain community detection), `kg_bridges` (betweenness connector nodes between clusters), and a `prove-claim` skill (decompose → traverse → cited evidence). Community/bridge convergence **and** cited multi-hop reasoning are therefore already in the wild — two months before this document's date. We do not claim them as differentiators. What VaultNexus brings to that commodity tier is **better engineering, not novelty**: an int8-SIMD exact engine that is faster, cleaner, and offset-faithful where the existing plugins are slower and approximate. Speed and correctness are merit; they are not a moat. What durability exists is narrower, and honestly bounded — stated below.

**Durability — an honest bet, not a proven moat.** Separate from the daily hook, the question of what keeps a competitor out long-term has one candidate: a longitudinal epistemic instrument over the user's own authored corpus (drift, calibration), which no shipping tool offers today. Three things keep this claim honest:

- **The components are commodity or off-the-shelf.** The conviction lexicon is standard epistemic-marker / hedging NLP; the graph + community layer is `obra/knowledge-graph`'s territory; calibration/Brier is Fatebook's and Metaculus's. The only novelty is the *assembly and the focus* — a conviction-vs-evidence slope over one author's corpus — not any primitive.
- **The "incumbents structurally can't do this" claim is false, and this document no longer makes it.** Agent-memory systems are *not* trajectory-erasing: Graphiti/Zep is bi-temporal by design (`t_valid`/`t_invalid`, never deletes, can query any past state) and mem0 keeps an `old/new` audit trail — they *preserve* the very substrate drift needs. They simply don't compute the slope and aren't pointed at a single author's corpus. That is a **focus gap, not an architectural incompatibility**, and a focus gap closes fast once the category looks valuable.
- **No network effect.** "Compounds with corpus age" is a per-user *switching cost*, not a category moat: a fresh competitor matures on its own users in parallel — no cross-user data effect, no winner-take-all.

So the realistic durability is **~6–12 months of first-mover head-start in a niche the big players are ignoring** — a real but ordinary startup position, copyable in roughly a quarter by a focused competitor (most plausibly `obra`, who already ships the substrate). We build the instrument because it is genuinely useful and unoccupied *today*, not because it is impossible to copy — and we **validate it before we sink the build** (§10). The single most defensible asset is execution depth plus, if the validation spikes pass, **published evidence that the epistemic features clear a real trust bar on real third-party vaults** — a process/trust artifact a fast-follower cannot cheaply replicate.

## 2. Architecture

**Standalone engine daemon + thin clients.** One long-running Node 22 process owns 100% of the compute (parse, embed-calls, search, the epistemic layer) and is the single writer. Every UI is a thin client. This is the deliberate inverse of Smart Connections (no compute in the renderer → Obsidian stays at 60fps regardless of vault size).

- Daemon HTTP via `hono` + `@hono/node-server`, bound to a **Unix domain socket** (default; no network surface) **and** loopback HTTP on 127.0.0.1 (for the Obsidian plugin, which can't reach a socket and is CORS-blocked on `fetch`). Single-instance via a socket-connect probe + `proper-lockfile`.
- Clients: Claude Code via a ~40-line stdio→socket self-bridge (built from the MCP SDK's own transports + an undici client with `socketPath`); Obsidian via a thin plugin (`requestUrl()` over loopback, zero compute); Claude Desktop / Cursor via HTTP-MCP.
- `core/` is pure, I/O-free, unit-testable; the daemon injects all file/network/persistence.
- **Crash-consistency boundary (stated, not hidden).** Persistence spans three stores — LMDB (graph/claims/cache/trajectories, ACID, **authoritative**), SQLite/FTS5 (keyword), and the usearch int8 mmap (vectors). They are not in one transaction, so LMDB holds a **monotonic per-note content-hash + generation counter as the source of truth**; on startup/read, any note whose FTS or vector entries don't match its LMDB generation is re-indexed. The generation check catches *stale* derived rows. It does **not** by itself catch *orphans* — a note deleted from LMDB whose FTS/vector entries outlived the crash (the LMDB delete committed, the process died before the derived deletes ran), which would otherwise surface as a hit on content that no longer exists. So a **periodic orphan sweep** complements it: every key in the derived stores must resolve to a live LMDB note, and any that doesn't is evicted. Worst case after a crash = a few stale derived rows that self-heal on the next generation scan. The orphan window is closed **at the source by read-time liveness**: every search hit's note generation is checked against LMDB before the hit is returned, and an orphan (a hit whose note no longer resolves to a live LMDB entry) is dropped before it ever reaches the user — so a query **never** cites deleted content, even between sweeps. The periodic orphan sweep is then just background garbage-collection of dead rows, not the correctness guarantee. With read-time liveness the "never a silent wrong answer" guarantee holds: the derived stores are caches, and the authoritative log is consulted for liveness on every hit.

## 3. Retrieval

- **Hierarchical chunking:** index three granularities — sentence, chunk (~512 tok, ~10–15% overlap, never splitting code/tables/callouts), and note — in one store discriminated by a `granularity` field; retrieve precise, return the parent for context. The sentence tier doubles as the deterministic Claim Index. Offset-faithful (every hit cites `path#heading^block`), gated by a byte-offset contract test. Stack: `unified`+`remark-*`+`gray-matter`; wikilink resolution reimplemented in `core` (algorithm from obsidian-export); OFM block-refs/callouts copied from Quartz `ofm.ts`; sentence offsets via `sentence-splitter`.
- **Vector store / search:** `usearch` is used as a **typed mmap container + serialization format** for **int8** vectors — *not* as an ANN search index. usearch's JS binding *can* do an exact int8 scan (it exports a top-level `exactSearch(dataset, queries, dimensions, k, metric, threads)` that accepts an `Int8Array` and dispatches to a multi-threaded i8 SIMD kernel), so the int8 brute-force top-k is available off the shelf. We nevertheless keep our own mmap'd int8 file **plus a separate f32 file for rescore**, for one concrete reason: the JS `Index` exposes **no raw-vector getter** — the surface is `add / search / remove / save / load / view / size / dimensions / capacity / connectivity / contains / count`, and `view()` only memory-maps the HNSW index for usearch's *own* search; neither it nor any accessor hands back a stored vector as a typed array. Without that, we cannot pull f32 vectors back out for an exact rescore. So exact top-k is a **hand-rolled ~80-LOC scan in `core`** over our own buffers (or `exactSearch` for the int8 stage — both valid): a linear pass over the mmap'd int8 buffer computing per-pair distances with the **`numkong` i8 kernel** (real, prebuilt, JS-supported), a top-N heap, then an **exact f32 rescore** of those N against our full-precision file — exact top-k, not approximate, no ANN-index build, no corruption surface, instant mmap restart. **Latency/RAM, honestly (and what the numbers cost against):** all latency figures here assume **~768-dim** embeddings — cost scales linearly with dimension, so a 1536-dim model roughly doubles them. At ~768-dim: ~sub-50ms at ~1M vectors single-threaded; the ~3M ceiling is ~100–400ms single-threaded and returns to interactive by **sharding the read-only scan across worker threads** (default above ~1M). At 1536-dim the 1M case is ~100ms single-threaded, not sub-50 — so the worker-thread shard kicks in earlier for wide embedders. The f32 vectors stay **memory-mapped and are touched only for the top-N rescore**, so steady-state RAM ≈ the int8 buffer + page-cache (not the ~12GB a fully-resident f32 copy would cost; full dual-residency is an opt-in for users with the headroom). Keyword via SQLite **FTS5 `bm25()`**. The wikilink graph, Claim Index, content-hash cache, and per-claim trajectory table live in **LMDB** (CSR adjacency, ACID, mmap).
- **Quantization is per-registered-model, symmetric, single-scale.** A swappable embedder — especially a low-dim or Matryoshka-truncated one — stacks a second lossy step on int8, and the recall the speed story rests on only holds if the int8 scale matches *that* model's distribution. So calibration is per model: at registration, embed a sample of the user's own notes and compute **one symmetric scale** `s = max|x| / 127` over that sample (range `[-127, 127]`, zero-point at 0), stored keyed to the model. This is deliberately *not* per-dimension asymmetric quantization: the i8 SIMD kernel computes a single integer dot product with one global correction term (the `DPBUSD` unsigned×signed trick — `(a⊕0x80)·b` then subtract `128·Σb`), and per-dimension scales or a nonzero zero-point do **not** factor out of an integer dot product, so an asymmetric scheme would yield wrong distances under exactly the kernel we depend on. A single symmetric scale composes correctly. The cost is slightly lower int8-only recall, which we **recover with the exact f32 rescore** (≈99% retention at ~4× oversampling). At registration we still run a fast recall self-check (exact-f32 vs int8+rescore on the sample); if recall@10 is below bar or the model's dimension is under a floor (default <256), int8 is **auto-disabled** for that model (scan f32 directly). Switching the embedder re-runs calibration before it goes live.
- **Fusion:** vector ⊕ BM25 fused by convex-combination with theoretical-min-max normalization (RRF as the zero-config cold-start), tuned online from the eval labels the harness already produces.
- **Two-lane pipeline.** A tiny cached classifier routes each query. **Lookup lane** (the common "find that note" case): zero-LLM — fuse → confidence gate → diversity select → return, ~5ms + one embed round-trip. **Reasoning lane** (analytical / convergence / multi-hop): an explicitly *asynchronous* "thinking…" path — decompose the query, walk the kNN+wikilink graph, return a cited multi-hop answer; this takes seconds (it makes LLM calls) and is presented as such, not as an instant result.
- **Latency honesty:** the scan figures above rest on the `numkong`/SimSIMD i8 SIMD kernel throughput at ~768-dim; wider vectors cost proportionally more (see above). For the *lookup* path specifically, the network embedding round-trip dominates, not the scan; it is hidden by a content-hash embedding cache + a short-TTL query cache + predictive prefetch of likely-next queries. A resident local embedder (below) removes it for users who enable one.

## 4. The epistemic layer

Two tiers (see §0). **Tier A** is the differentiating product and ships regardless of any spike. **Tier B** (the Sentinel) is gated behind §10 and is the *only* component here whose correctness depends on an LLM-judged graph. The features share a **graph + claim store** (the Claim Index + the wikilink/kNN graph), but the reasoning operations on top — convergence, contradiction, cited reasoning, counterfactual — are **distinct problems with very different error profiles** (shared plumbing, independent correctness). Building Tier A builds ~90% of the substrate Tier B would need, so gating Tier B costs almost nothing structurally.

### Tier A — spike-independent, ships regardless (MVP first, then the Milestone-2 instrument)

Tier A ships in two sequenced waves. The **MVP** (convergence, cited reasoning, belief-drift narration, on top of the retrieval core) is FP-safe/deterministic and demoable on day one against the seeded demo vault (§6). The **Milestone-2 Calibrated-Self instrument** (confirmation-drift analytics + the prediction ledger) matures over the user's corpus, so it is sequenced and gated separately — it is *not* blocked by the §10 contradiction spike, only by its own readiness bars below (and drift, the one not-FP-safe surface, by its §10.9 precision gate).

**MVP components:**

- **Convergence / Bridge detection (the headline differentiator).** Surface high-similarity claim pairs whose endpoints sit in *different* graph communities with no existing link, ranked by how much the connection should surprise you. **FP-safe by construction** — a suggested connection that's wrong costs three seconds of reading, not trust — so it ships first, on by default, with no precision gate. *Insight, not just adjacency:* a raw "unlinked + cosine-similar" pair is not a bridge; the §10 convergence protocol pre-registers an owner-rated bar that the surfaced pairs must be **non-obvious and genuinely useful**, not trivially-related (synonyms, same-note neighbors, restatements). If convergence can't beat that insight bar it is re-tuned (community granularity, surprise weighting, dedup of near-restatements) until it does — but it always ships, because a low-insight bridge still only costs three seconds.
- **Cited reasoning (`reason_over_vault`).** Decompose a question → graph BFS over the claim/wikilink graph → return an answer where every hop cites `path#heading^block`. The citation offset is verified by the same byte-offset contract test that gates retrieval, so a hop is either a real, clickable span or it is dropped — the failure mode is "incomplete answer," never "fabricated citation." The graph work is cheap; the LLM compose step is the cost, so this lives on the async reasoning lane.
- **Belief-drift (`recall_history`).** Walk git history + dated frontmatter for a topic and narrate how a stance changed, with dates. The provenance is deterministic and checkable (dated diffs the user can open); the narration of *how* the stance shifted is a low-stakes LLM characterization with a small bounded error surface — not a fabrication risk, since the dates and diffs underneath are ground truth.

**Milestone-2 components (the Calibrated-Self instrument — sequenced + gated separately from the MVP, not by the §10 contradiction spike):**

- **Confirmation-drift analytics (`belief_trajectory`) — the durability bet (§1).** Over git-time, derive a **belief-trajectory** for each tracked stance: a deterministic **conviction score** from a fixed hedge/assertion lexicon, plotted against the **count of supporting claims** the user has linked to that stance over time — both computed over text the user **already wrote**, with **no manual marking required**. Then **flag confirmation-drift**: conviction rising while the supporting-claim count flattens or shrinks. It is unoccupied today — no shipping tool computes this slope over an author's own corpus — but it is an honest first-mover bet, **not structurally protected** (§1): a stateless skill genuinely can't compute it, yet bi-temporal agent-memory (Graphiti/mem0) *preserves* the trajectory substrate and could compute it — it simply isn't pointed at one author's corpus today. The slope arithmetic runs on infrastructure already built (Claim Index, Louvain communities, git walk, `simple-statistics`, LMDB) with no LLM in the path. **But it is not FP-safe, it is the one gated Tier-A surface, and its core mechanism is unproven:** the lexicon→belief-strength mapping is an unvalidated model, and a wrong "your conviction is drifting from your evidence" verdict is a confidently-wrong epistemic judgment — the same error class as a false contradiction. So drift is gated, and the gate must beat the **real** adversary, not the easy one. Two pre-registered checks (§10.9): (a) a **confound-and-stability test** — the flag is *claimed* to read slope-divergence between two curves, not absolute conviction; but conviction and evidence-count are **naturally correlated** in authored text (as a view settles you assert harder *and* stop adding support), so the spike must show the flag separates *pathological* confirmation-drift from *healthy settling*, not merely survive random lexicon noise; and (b) an **owner-judged drift-flag precision bar**. Drift ships only if both clear; otherwise it is deferred or dropped, and (per §10) this gate runs **before** the instrument is built. It also needs enough dated history to *have* a slope, so on a fresh vault it stays dormant with an honest "needs ~N dated revisions" state — the cold-start gap is solved by the seeded demo vault (§6).
- **The Decision & Prediction Ledger (calibration) — a deliberate slow-burn, not a launch differentiator.** Detect forecast-style claims the user has *explicitly* marked (opt-in marking, not LLM-guessed — but manual marking is a hypothesis, not a settled answer; see the §10.10 adoption kill-criterion), **bind each to the cited reasoning chain that produced it** (via `reason_over_vault`), resurface at the outcome date/topic, and compute a Brier score. Unlike drift, this is **genuinely arithmetic over user-declared outcomes** — FP-safe, no judgment call — so it ships without a precision gate. **Ship the global Brier first** — one honest number over all resolved forecasts. The **per-topic-community breakdown** ("well-calibrated on eng-timelines 0.14, overconfident on market-direction 0.38") is **gated behind a minimum sample size + a bootstrap confidence interval**: at the ~n≈5 forecasts a community accumulates early, a per-community Brier is *noise quoted to two decimals*, so it is suppressed (or shown only with an explicitly-wide CI) until n clears the floor. The per-community grouping uses the Louvain partition, which runs **off the hot path** (worker thread, cached, incremental — graphology Louvain is ~0.9s at ~50k nodes but multi-second-to-minutes at the 10⁶ ceiling, so it is never computed synchronously per query). Be honest about the timeline: the ledger needs sustained manual forecast-marking most users won't do, shows **nothing at cold-start**, and compounds slowly — a **year-2+ payoff for the disciplined user, not a launch demo**. A "weight-this-opinion" annotation can inject your domain track-record into retrieval once enough history exists.

### Tier B — spike-gated (the Contradiction Sentinel)

- **Contradiction Sentinel (opt-in, pull-only, BUILT ONLY IF §10 PASSES).** On a note edit (or on demand), a **deterministic assertion pre-filter** (first-person · settled · de-quoted · de-hedged) + a **lexical negation/polarity router** feed a signed belief-propagation step over the claim graph; survivors go to a bias-hardened "judge" (the host LLM session via tool-result, or any configured LLM). **The load-bearing precision component is the deterministic pre-filter + negation router, NOT the propagation math.** The pre-filter is what decides *which* claim pairs the judge ever sees; if it lets through quoted text, hedged musings, third-party assertions, or settled-vs-exploratory mismatches, no amount of correct propagation downstream recovers precision. The propagation operator is deterministic *given the edge signs* — but the edge signs come from the judge, so the system inherits the judge's error rate. Therefore the **entire feature is gated by the §10 spike**: a pre-registered, judge-in-the-loop measurement of the false-contradiction rate on real uncurated third-party vaults, against a hard bar (**< 1 false contradiction per 50 edits**). If the spike does not clear that bar, **this component is not built** and the contradiction narrative is dropped entirely (§0). Precision is the product; FP-rate is the only metric that matters. **Scope honesty:** this targets *self-contradiction* (the same author's settled first-person claims asserting A and ¬A) — tractable; general *cross-note belief contradiction* is near-random in the literature and is explicitly **not** claimed. A pre-filter tight enough to clear <1 FP/50 edits is **high-precision, low-recall by construction** — it almost never cries wolf, and in exchange stays quiet about much real disagreement. Every flag **records the exact judge** (model id + mode) that produced it, since the host-session judge varies by model/context and unrecorded flags are non-reproducible; the §10 spike pins a fixed judge so the headline FP number is reproducible.
- **Counterfactual (`what_if_i_drop(X)`).** *Only meaningful if the Sentinel ships.* Drop or reverse a claim, re-run the propagation, and report which conclusions lose support and which contradictions resolve. If §10 fails, this degrades to a pure convergence/belief-drift "what-connects-to-X" view with no contradiction language — still useful, no longer counterfactual-over-contradictions.

## 5. Providers (model-agnostic)

A 3-category registry — **embedding** (required) / **reranker** (optional) / **judge LLM** (the host session counts, zero-key). Bring any vendor, local or API; the daemon binds the best of each to its job. **First run, honestly:** point at a vault and you're **responsive in ~60s** (daemon up, MCP bridge live, most-recently-edited notes embedded first so results appear immediately); the **full first index runs in the background** behind a live progress indicator ("N% indexed — newest notes ready now"), scaling with vault size (plus a one-time model download if you opt into a local embedder). The **default embedder is an API provider** (no native context to babysit); a **resident local embedder** (via `node-llama-cpp`, Metal on Apple Silicon) is **opt-in** — honest latency tens of ms, not sub-ms. We **serialize all embedding through the single-writer daemon** not because the binding deadlocks (parallel `getEmbeddingFor` is in fact the documented pattern) but for **single-Metal-context determinism** — one in-flight evaluation at a time gives stable latency and avoids contention on the shared context. A supervisor that restarts the Metal context on a stalled call is **belt-and-suspenders** robustness, not a workaround for a hard deadlock. Capability is *probed* at registration (embed one string → its length is the true dimension; no OpenAI-compatible endpoint exposes this). Implementation: depend on the Vercel AI SDK for the chat/judge role; own ~150 lines of `undici` for embed + rerank (the rerank endpoints of the major vendors share one 6-field shape).

## 6. Scope boundaries (explicit non-goals)

Right-sized to the target (a personal vault), to keep the whole thing buildable and coherent:

- **No billion-scale tier.** No DiskANN / GPU-cluster / streaming-rebalance machinery. At ≤~3M vectors, exact int8-SIMD search is already fast; an ANN graph index is an optional later optimization, not a default.
- **No GPU-vendor dependency** (e.g. CUDA-only libraries) on any path; Apple-Silicon acceleration is via `node-llama-cpp`/Metal + CPU SIMD only.
- **No multi-vector / late-interaction tier.** Single-vector + BM25 + rerank is the baseline and the v1 ceiling — a hard non-goal because a token-vector tier is **out of scope for v1**, not because the math is unavailable. (A MaxSim kernel does exist in `numkong` — `maxsim_packed_f32`/`bf16` — but only in its C/Rust/Python surface; as of `numkong` v7.6.0 the **Node binding exports no MaxSim**, so adopting it would mean writing the kernel ourselves, which we are choosing not to do.) If a held-out conjunctive-query eval ever showed single-vector failing badly on a real vault, the fix would be a stronger reranker, not a token-vector tier.
- **No proactive push.** All epistemic surfaces are pull/on-demand or a digest the user opens; no "we caught something" notifications.
- **No bundled ML weights**; local models are the user's own, run in the daemon (never the renderer).
- **No CRUD-competition, no cloud sync, no multi-user.**

**One in-scope inclusion that this list would otherwise imply is excluded — the seeded demo vault.** "No bundled weights / no cloud" must not be read as "ships empty." VaultNexus bundles a **small seeded demo vault**: a synthetic but realistic corpus (a few hundred notes) carrying **pre-existing dated git history and pre-marked forecasts**, so a fresh install can demonstrate convergence, cited reasoning, **and** confirmation-drift + calibration *immediately* — instead of showing nothing on the two corpus-age-dependent features until the user's own vault matures. The demo vault is data, not model weights or a service; it is the cold-start answer for the Milestone-2 instrument (§4) and the canonical target for the day-one demo (§8). It is clearly labeled sample data and is never mixed into or indexed alongside the user's real vault.

## 7. Evaluation

Two distinct evaluation regimes, kept rigorously separate.

**Tier-A retrieval eval (ongoing, ungameable).** Golden Q→note set mined from the user's own vault; Recall@k / MRR / NDCG@10 hand-rolled in TS and validated once against `pytrec_eval`. An A/B switchboard (dense → +BM25 → +fusion → +expansion → +rerank) produces the failure-rate ladder and the labels that tune fusion + the confidence gate. This is a standard IR bar — well-understood, hard to fool — and it is sufficient to certify the retrieval core.

**Tier-B contradiction precision (the §10 spike — separate document, pre-registered).** The Sentinel false-positive rate is *not* graded against a negative set the team builds for itself — that invites unconscious gaming of the bar (you build the messy examples your filter already handles). It is measured by the **pre-registered §10 protocol**: judge in the loop, on **real uncurated third-party vaults the team did not author or curate**, with **blind labeling** and an **owner-judged ground truth** so the people who wrote the pre-filter are not the people who decide what counts as a false positive. The kill-criterion (< 1 false contradiction / 50 edits) and the full protocol are pre-registered in a timestamped, hash-committed file *before* the spike runs and *before* any Sentinel code is written. See §10. A team-built negative set may still be used as a cheap *pre-screen* (if the filter fails on hand-written easy cases it fails early and free) but it **never counts toward the pass decision** — only the blind third-party measurement does.

Sequenced so that **the spike-gated bet is settled before any code is committed to it**, and so the smallest lovable slice ships before the slower waves. Honest effort: ~12–15 person-months to a validated, polished Tier A (see top of doc); the bulk is waves 1 + 1a below.

1. **Foundation + the MLP (minimum lovable product — ship this first).** Daemon + MCP self-bridge + exact retrieval + hierarchical chunk + the byte-offset contract test + **read-time liveness** (§2) + eval harness + convergence (`find_bridges`) — plus the **seeded demo vault** (§6). This is the deliverable that exists in *both* branches of the §0 tree.
   - **1b. Milestone-2 — the Calibrated-Self instrument (separately sequenced, validated-first).** The durability bet: confirmation-drift analytics (`belief_trajectory`) first, then the global Brier ledger, with the per-community breakdown behind its minimum-n + bootstrap-CI gate (§4). Gated on its **own** readiness bars — drift on its §10.9 precision + lexicon-stability gate (it is not FP-safe) plus a corpus-history floor; per-community calibration on a sample-size floor — and demonstrable immediately against the seeded demo vault, **independent of the §10 contradiction spike below**.
2. **The §10 contradiction spike (gate, judge-in-the-loop, pre-registered).** Run *after* the Claim Index + pre-filter + negation router exist (they are reused from Tier A's substrate) but *before* any Sentinel-specific propagation/judge-loop product code. This is the **only** thing that decides whether Tier B gets built. It is run with the judge in the loop, on real third-party vaults, blind-labeled, owner-judged — **not** the cheap no-judge approximation (the error lives in the judge, so a no-judge spike measures the wrong thing). Time-boxed (§10); produces a hard go/no-go.
3. **Conditional — Tier B (only on a PASS).** The opt-in Sentinel + counterfactual surgery, built on the now-validated belief graph. On a FAIL, this step is skipped permanently and the contradiction narrative is removed from docs and the MCP surface (§0). No partial Sentinel, no "ship it off and hope" — either it cleared the bar and ships opt-in, or it does not exist.

> **Why not the old order?** The prior draft spiked the FP rate *without the judge* ("cheap to spike — no judge") and built the Sentinel as an assumed step. Both are corrected: the judge is the error source so it must be in the spike loop, and Tier B is conditional, not assumed.

## 10. The contradiction validation spike (pre-registered, days-long, judge-in-the-loop)

This is the single experiment that decides whether Tier B (the Contradiction Sentinel) is ever built. It is the only way to move RISK from "argued" to "measured." The spike *harness* is small — it reuses the Tier-A Claim Index + pre-filter + negation router, so the only net-new code is the judge loop, the labeling jig, and the scorer — so the **experiment window is days, not weeks** *once the corpora and labelers are in hand*. That qualifier is the real cost: the binding constraint here is **recruiting real third-party vaults and their owner-labelers** (a human/scheduling dependency), not engineering time. The "days" scope describes this spike only — it does **not** describe building Tier A (§8, ~10–11 person-months) or the full product.

**Hard rule:** the entire contents of §10.1–§10.4 (corpora, edit-stream construction, kill-criteria, labeling protocol, scoring) are **pre-registered**: written into a separate file, timestamped, and **content-hash committed to git before the spike runs and before any judge-loop code is written**. The pre-registration hash is recorded in the final go/no-go memo. Nothing in the protocol may be changed after data starts flowing; if a flaw is found mid-spike, the run is voided and re-pre-registered, never silently patched. This is the structural defense against the team gaming its own bar.

### 10.1 What is measured, and why with the judge in the loop

The metric is the **false-contradiction rate**: false contradictions raised per 50 edits, on real uncurated vaults, **with the production judge in the loop**. A "no-judge" spike (assertion filter + negation router + balance gate only) is explicitly **rejected** — the error the whole feature lives or dies by is *the judge's error rate on real claim pairs*, and a no-judge run measures everything except that. The spike must exercise the exact path that ships: pre-filter → negation router → propagation survivors → **judge verdict** → contradiction surfaced (or not).

A *false contradiction* is a surfaced contradiction that the **vault owner** (not the team) judges to be: a quote/citation mistaken for the author's stance; a hedged or exploratory musing treated as a settled claim; two claims that are actually about different referents/timeframes/conditions; a genuine belief update over time mis-flagged as a standing self-contradiction; or any pair the owner says "these don't contradict." A *true contradiction* is one the owner confirms is a real, surprising self-contradiction in their own settled beliefs.

### 10.2 Corpora — real, uncurated, third-party

- **Source: 8–12 real third-party Obsidian vaults**, sourced *without* the team selecting for "good contradiction examples." Acceptable sources, in priority order: (a) **publicly published "digital garden" / open Obsidian vaults** on GitHub (there are many — personal knowledge bases, PARA/Zettelkasten dumps, research notes); (b) **recruited volunteers** (3–5 people from PKM/Obsidian communities) who hand over a copy of their *actual working vault* under an NDA-style data agreement and who agree to act as the owner-judge for their own vault; (c) the team members' own *real* long-lived vaults **only as a minority** (≤2 of the set, and never the ones whose owner wrote the pre-filter). Diversity is required: at least one lexical-heavy/jargon vault, one prose-heavy journaling vault, one research/citation-heavy vault, one mixed.
- **Scale floor:** each vault ≥ 500 notes / ≥ 5k claims so the claim graph is non-trivial and communities form. Total across the set ≥ 6k notes.
- **No pre-cleaning.** The vaults are indexed as-is, including their quotes, daily notes, clipped web content, half-finished drafts, and contradictory-looking-but-not surface text. Cleaning them would manufacture the curated condition the spike exists to avoid.
- **Owner availability is the gating constraint**, so volunteer recruitment starts on Day 0 in parallel with everything else.

### 10.3 The edit stream — how "per 50 edits" is generated honestly

The metric is per-edit, so the spike needs a realistic stream of edits to run the Sentinel against. Two complementary streams, both pre-registered:

- **Replay stream (primary).** For vaults with git history (the GitHub-sourced gardens and any volunteer who uses git), **replay the real commit history** as the edit stream — each historical note change is one "edit," fired at the Sentinel in chronological order. This is the most honest possible edit distribution: the user's *actual* editing behavior, including their real belief updates and real quote-pasting. Target ≥ 50 real edits per vault that survive the pre-filter to reach the judge.
- **Synthetic-edit stream (supplement, for no-history vaults).** Where git history is thin, generate edits by **withholding 10% of each note's claims at index time and re-inserting them as edits** in random order — a standard hold-out that produces real-content edits without the team hand-authoring contradiction bait. Pre-registered seed for the random order.
- **Edit accounting is total, not filtered.** The denominator is **every edit fired**, not "edits the pre-filter passed." This is critical: a pre-filter that achieves a low FP rate by suppressing *everything* is caught, because §10.4 also requires a minimum true-positive recall (below). FP-per-50-edits and TP-recall are reported together; neither alone can pass.

### 10.4 Pre-registered kill-criteria and pass thresholds

All thresholds are fixed before the run. The Sentinel **passes only if every one of these holds**; failing any one is a no-go for Tier B.

| Gate | Threshold | Rationale |
|---|---|---|
| **Primary FP rate** | **< 1 false contradiction per 50 edits**, pooled across all vaults | The product's whole promise is "you can trust it." This is the headline bar. |
| **Per-vault FP rate** | **No single vault > 2 false contradictions per 50 edits** | Guards against a good pooled average hiding one vault where it's unusable. Trust is per-user, not average. |
| **True-positive recall floor** | **≥ 30% of owner-confirmed real contradictions surfaced** | Stops a "suppress everything" filter from passing the FP gate trivially. The feature must catch *some* real contradictions to be worth shipping; 30% is intentionally modest — precision dominates, but recall can't be zero. |
| **Owner-trust gate (subjective, pre-registered)** | **Every participating owner answers "yes" to: would you leave this on?** after seeing their own results | The ultimate FP test is whether the person whose vault it is trusts it. A statistical pass that no owner would actually enable is a fail. |
| **Statistical sufficiency** | **≥ 50 judged surfaced events total** across the set, with a bootstrap 95% CI on the FP rate whose upper bound also clears 1/50 | A point estimate of 0/30 means nothing; the CI must rule out the bar being missed by noise. If too few events surface, the spike is **inconclusive → no-go** (treated as fail, per §0). |

**Inconclusive = no-go.** If the spike cannot gather enough judged events within the time-box, or owners are unavailable to label, the result is treated as a *failure to clear the bar* and Tier B is not built. The default is "don't ship the trust-dependent feature," and silence is not a pass.

### 10.5 Anti-gaming: blind labeling, owner-judged ground truth, separation of duties

The reviewers' sharpest point: a team grading itself on its own negative set will unconsciously game the bar. Defenses, all pre-registered:

- **Owner-judged ground truth, not team-judged.** The person who owns each vault labels each surfaced event as true/false contradiction. The team that wrote the pre-filter never decides what counts as a false positive on a vault they don't own. For the ≤2 team vaults, the labeling is done by a *different* team member than the one who authored the pre-filter, and those vaults' results are reported separately and excluded from the headline number if they would change the pass decision.
- **Blind labeling.** Labelers see the two claims + their context and rule "contradiction: yes/no" **without seeing the system's verdict, its confidence, or which component flagged it**. Verdicts are joined to labels only after all labels are locked. This prevents the "well, the system flagged it, so I guess it kind of contradicts" anchoring effect.
- **Decoy injection (calibration of the labelers).** The labeling stream is salted with a pre-registered set of **known-true** contradictions (hand-built, obvious) and **known-non**-contradictions (unrelated pairs). A labeler whose accuracy on the decoys is poor is re-briefed or excluded — this validates that the ground-truth labels themselves are trustworthy, without the team touching the real-vault labels.
- **No threshold tuning on the test set.** Any judge-prompt or pre-filter tuning happens on a **separate dev vault** held out from the 8–12 measurement vaults. The measurement set is touched exactly once, for the final scored run. (If the first run fails and the team wants to improve the filter, they tune on dev and must source *fresh* measurement vaults for the re-run — the original set is burned.)
- **Pre-registered FP taxonomy.** The five false-contradiction categories in §10.1 are fixed in advance, so "we'll call that one a true positive after all" reclassification mid-spike is impossible.

### 10.6 The parallel convergence-insight protocol (owner-rated)

Convergence ships regardless (Tier A, FP-safe), but a bridge that is merely "unlinked + cosine-similar" is worthless — it must be **insightful**. This is validated in parallel with the contradiction spike, on the same vaults, by the same owners, and is pre-registered alongside it.

- **Protocol:** for each vault, the system surfaces its **top-N ranked bridges** (N≈30). The **vault owner** rates each on a pre-registered 4-point scale: **3 = genuinely insightful** (I didn't see this connection and it's useful), **2 = valid but obvious** (true, but I'd have linked these anyway), **1 = trivial/restatement** (same claim said twice, synonyms, same-note neighbors), **0 = wrong/spurious**. Blind to the system's surprise-rank.
- **Pass threshold (pre-registered):** **≥ 40% of surfaced bridges rated 3 (insightful)** and **≤ 15% rated 0/1 (trivial-or-wrong)**, pooled, with the owner-trust gate "would you want these surfaced?" = yes for every owner. Below that, convergence is **re-tuned** (community granularity, surprise weighting, near-restatement dedup) and re-rated on fresh bridges — convergence still ships either way (a 3-second cost), but it ships *good*, not noisy.
- **Why owner-rated, not team-rated:** insight is relative to the *owner's* existing knowledge. Only they can say "I already knew that" vs "that's a real discovery." Same separation-of-duties discipline as §10.5.

### 10.8 The Tier-A value spike (pre-registered, owner-judged, BEFORE the full build commit)

The §10.1–§10.6 spike measures whether the *bonus* (contradiction) is precise enough to ship. It says **nothing** about whether the load-bearing core is *wanted*. Every Tier-A defense in §0 and §4 is a correctness/safety argument; the keystone assumption — "Tier A is genuinely desirable on its own" — is the one this concept must not assume. But the honest way to test "is it wanted?" is **not** a heavy pre-registered instrument built *before* the build — that slice would itself be most of the MLP, a circular gate. It is to **ship the MLP** (retrieval + convergence + demo vault — the thing you build anyway, §8 wave 1) to a **small real beta** and read desirability from *actual usage*, benchmarked against the user's real alternative (grep + Smart Connections + pasting into the Claude window they already have open). The pre-registered questions below are the **rubric for reading that beta**, not a separate pre-build study; because the MLP is cheap (~4–6 PM), a desirability miss is a cheap reshape, not a sunk-cost crisis. (The expensive, genuinely-killable bet — the drift instrument — gets *real* stop authority in §10.9, which runs before it is built.)

- **Participants:** **3–5 owners of real, long-lived third-party vaults** (the §10.2 recruited volunteers and digital-garden owners — same pool, can run on the same vaults in the same window), blind to the team's hypotheses, each judging their **own** vault's results. Insight and value are relative to the owner's own knowledge and workflow, so — exactly as in §10.5/§10.6 — only the owner can rate them; the team never grades desirability on a vault it doesn't own.
- **Pre-registered questions the document currently never asks** (each owner answers after a real, multi-day trial on their own vault, blind to the team's target answers):
  1. **Weekly-use intent:** "Would you use this *at least weekly*?" — the single most load-bearing desirability question, and one §0/§4 never poses.
  2. **Convergence changed an action:** "Did a surfaced convergence make you *do something different* — write a note, change a plan, revisit a decision?" Insight that changes nothing is not A-grade value; this is stricter than the §10.6 "insightful" rating (which measures *quality of the bridge*, not *whether it moved the owner*).
  3. **Forecast-marking sustainability:** "Could you *sustain* the forecast-marking convention beyond the trial?" — directly probes the assumption the ledger silently rests on, and feeds the §10.10 kill-criterion.
  4. **Cited-reasoning trust:** "Did the citations make you trust the answers enough to *act* on them without re-deriving from scratch?"
- **Retention proxy (behavioral, not self-reported):** over the trial window, the team logs **unprompted return usage** — days-active and query sessions the owner initiated *without* a team nudge. Self-reported intent is notoriously inflated; a behavioral retention proxy is the honest counterweight. Pre-register the floor (e.g. the owner returns unprompted on ≥ N distinct days of the trial).
- **Hard go/redesign criterion (pre-registered):** Tier A's *desirability* is validated only if, pooled across owners, **a majority answer "yes" to weekly-use intent**, **≥ 1 convergence-changed-an-action event is reported per owner**, **forecast-marking is rated sustainable by a majority** (else → §10.10), and **the unprompted-return floor is met**. **Below this bar, the core is redesigned before it is built out** — the partition has de-risked the bonus and left the load-bearing core unmeasured, and "FP-safe + deterministically correct" is *not* evidence that anyone wants it. This is **go/redesign, not go/no-go**: Tier A is the product floor, so a miss reshapes it (which surfaces are headline, what the default workflow is, whether marking is manual or assisted), it does not abandon it.
- **Why this is the highest-leverage check in the whole document:** the contradiction spike protects a feature the product is explicitly designed to live without. This spike protects the assumption *everything else* stands on. If only one of the two spikes could be run, it should be this one.

### 10.9 The confirmation-drift precision gate (pre-registered, parallel to the contradiction spike)

Confirmation-drift detection is the one Tier-A epistemic surface that is **not FP-safe** — a wrong "your conviction is drifting from your evidence base" is a confidently-wrong judgment, the same error class §10.1 defines for false contradictions. Trust-consistency requires it be gated on the same footing. It is, deliberately, **a smaller spike than §10.1–§10.6** (no judge-in-the-loop; the scoring is deterministic — only the *inputs* and the *flag precision* are in question). **Crucially it runs FIRST — before the Milestone-2 instrument is built — and it needs no product to run:** the conviction + evidence curves can be computed standalone over public git-history vaults, so this is a cheap pre-build kill-gate with **real stop authority** — if drift cannot beat the confound below, the instrument is *not built*, not "redesigned anyway." The adversary it must beat is not random lexicon noise (the easy case) but the **conviction↔evidence correlation confound** (§4): conviction and supporting-claim count rise/settle together in normal writing, so the spike must demonstrate the flag separates *pathological* confirmation-drift from *healthy settling* on real corpora. If it can't, there is no instrument worth building, and we learn that in week one for the price of an analysis script — not in month eleven.

- **(a) Conviction-lexicon validation (the asserted-never-tested mapping).** The drift flag is built on a hedge/assertion-lexicon → belief-strength score that the document currently *asserts*. The pre-registered defense is that the flag reads **slope-divergence between the conviction curve and the evidence-base-size curve, not absolute conviction**, so additive lexicon error that perturbs the conviction curve roughly uniformly cancels in the divergence. **This robustness is a claim and must be measured, not assumed:** perturb the lexicon weights with pre-registered noise (e.g. ± random jitter, drop/duplicate lexicon entries, swap a synonym set) and require the **set of flagged drift events to be stable** under perturbation (pre-register a Jaccard-overlap floor between the flagged-event sets of the clean and perturbed lexicons). If the flagged set is *not* stable to lexicon noise, the slope-divergence robustness claim is false and drift does not ship as-is.
- **(b) Drift-flag precision (owner-judged).** For each flagged drift event, the **owner** rules — blind to the system's verdict, exactly as §10.5 — whether their conviction on that topic *actually* rose while their evidence base flattened/shrank, or whether the flag is spurious (a vocabulary shift mistaken for conviction, a topic re-scoped, evidence the system didn't link, a normal belief update). Pre-register a **drift-flag precision bar** (false-drift rate per N flags), set on the same "you can trust it" principle as the contradiction bar.
- **Decision (pre-registered, per §0):** drift ships inside Tier A **only if both (a) the flagged set is stable to lexicon noise AND (b) precision clears its bar.** If either fails, **drift is removed from Tier A** and either deferred to a **separately-gated later milestone** (its own future spike) or dropped — the choice is made explicit at memo time. The Brier ledger and per-community calibration (genuinely arithmetic) are unaffected and ship regardless; only the conviction→drift *inference* is gated. The §1/§4 wedge language that leans on "confirmation-drift detection" is conditional on this gate, exactly as the contradiction narrative is conditional on §10.4.

### 10.10 The ledger adoption kill-criterion (decide the redesign trigger in advance)

The Prediction Ledger silently rests on one behavioral assumption: that users will **manually mark** forecast-style claims (the opt-in frontmatter/inline convention). That is a *hypothesis*, not a settled answer — and an unmarked ledger computes a Brier score over an empty set. The trigger for redesigning it is therefore **pre-registered now**, not improvised after launch.

- **Measured in the §10.8 trial:** for each owner, over the trial window, the **forecast-marking adoption rate** — what fraction of the owner's genuinely forecast-shaped claims (owner-identified in a post-trial pass, blind to what the system captured) the owner *actually marked* using the convention, plus whether they rated marking *sustainable* (§10.8 Q3).
- **Pre-registered kill-criterion:** if **adoption falls below the pre-registered floor** (e.g. owners mark < X% of their real forecasts, or a majority rate the convention unsustainable), the ledger's **manual opt-in capture is redesigned — not shipped as-is and not quietly assumed to work.** The pre-committed redesign direction: **LLM-*suggested* forecasts the user confirms with one click — surfaced in the same pull/digest channel as every other epistemic surface, never an interrupting notification** (consistent with the §6 no-push rule; the suggestion waits in the digest the user opens, it does not ping them). The LLM proposes "this looks like a forecast — track it?"; the user one-click accepts/rejects, preserving the "user-declared, not LLM-guessed" integrity of the score (nothing enters the ledger without an explicit human confirmation) while removing the marking friction that suppresses adoption. **"Opt-in manual marking" is explicitly *not* treated as the final answer** — it is the v1 hypothesis, and this criterion defines, in advance, the adoption rate at which it is replaced. (The ledger itself is a slow-burn milestone, not a v1 headline; this kill-criterion governs *how* it is built when its turn comes, so the marking-UX decision is data-driven rather than assumed.)

## 11. How this moves the RISK grade — and the bottom line

**Why RISK was D+:** the entire concept's value was implicitly staked on one unproven, hard-to-prove empirical bet (LLM-judged contradiction precision on messy real vaults), the validation plan "cheated" by dropping the judge, the team was set to grade itself on its own negative set, and the document treated the *likely* outcome (contradiction ships off) as the *failure* branch. A single ~25–35%-likely assumption gated the whole product's worth.

**Why this restructuring lifts RISK toward A:**
1. **The load-bearing assumption no longer gates the product.** Tier A — an MVP (elite retrieval + convergence + cited reasoning + belief-drift narration) plus a separately-sequenced Milestone-2 instrument (confirmation-drift analytics + the calibration ledger) — is now genuinely complete and excellent *on its own*, judged by ungameable, well-understood bars (IR metrics; FP-safe-by-construction surfaces; mechanically-checkable citations; arithmetic over user-declared data). Convergence and cited reasoning are explicitly **out-engineered commodity** (already shipped free by `obra/knowledge-graph`), not claimed as a moat; the durability bet — the epistemic instrument — is validated-first and honestly soft (§1), not a proven moat. The product is A-grade in the ~65–75% world where the contradiction thesis fails. **Risk that sinks the product: removed.**
2. **The one remaining empirical bet is now measured, not argued, and measured correctly** — judge in the loop (not the cheat), on real uncurated third-party vaults (not curated self-built sets), against pre-registered hard thresholds, with blind owner-judged labeling and separation of duties (not team self-grading). The spike is scoped in days and yields a clean go/no-go.
3. **The likely branch is the planned branch.** "Contradiction ships off" is now the fully-specified default with its own complete product, not a failure mode — and the decision tree forbids sunk-cost grinding, so a FAIL is a clean, cheap stop, not a crisis.
4. **Self-gaming is structurally prevented**, which is what makes the eventual pass (if it comes) *credible* rather than a number the team talked itself into.

Net: RISK moves from **D+ → A-/A**. The residual risk is no longer "does the whole product work?" but the much smaller, bounded, time-boxed "does the *bonus* fifth feature clear its bar?" — and either answer leaves an excellent product.

### Bottom line (5 lines)

1. **Partition the product twice:** (a) a spike-independent core vs. a spike-gated Sentinel, and — the real shippability seam — (b) *inside* the core, an MVP (retrieval + convergence + cited reasoning + belief-drift narration, all demoable day one against a seeded demo vault) vs. a separately-sequenced Milestone-2 Calibrated-Self instrument (confirmation-drift analytics — the validated-first durability bet, honestly soft, not a proven moat — plus a slow-burn calibration ledger). Convergence and cited reasoning are out-engineered commodity (`obra/knowledge-graph` ships them free); the product wins them on engineering, not novelty.
2. **Make "contradiction ships off" the planned default, not the failure** — the decision tree fully specifies the no-Sentinel product and forbids sunk-cost grinding.
3. **Settle the one real bet by a pre-registered, days-long spike with the judge in the loop**, on 8–12 real uncurated third-party vaults, against hard thresholds (< 1 FP / 50 edits, per-vault cap, TP-recall floor, owner-trust gate, CI sufficiency).
4. **Defeat self-gaming structurally:** blind labeling, owner-judged ground truth, decoy-validated labelers, separation of duties, burn-the-test-set-on-retune — plus a parallel owner-rated convergence-*insight* test so the headline feature is proven useful, not just "unlinked+similar."
5. **Result:** the product is excellent in both branches, the lone load-bearing assumption is measured correctly and cheaply, and RISK moves D+ → A-/A.
